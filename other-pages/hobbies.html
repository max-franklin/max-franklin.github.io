Camping and Hiking

Anchorage has opportunities aplenty for getting outside, many of which can be done as day hikes. Most of my favorite hikes are in the front range of the Chugach, including Wolverine Peak, O'Malley Peak, and of course, the Anchorage favorite, Flat Top Mountain. Wolverine in particular is one of my favorites, with its excellent view back into its valleys on either side.

Day hikes are an awesome way to quickly get out and enjoy the sites around Anchorage, but if that's not enough, it's easy to make a weekend out of many of Anchorage's trails. One of my favorite backpacking trails is the Williwaw Lakes trail. I love it for its parallel walk beside the peaks going behind Wolverine. In fact, if you look carefully at the photo above, you can just make out the beginning of Williwaw Lakes in the valley behind and on the right. If you're willing to drive a little bit, my all time favorite backpacking trips are closer to Seward, Alaska: the Carter Lake Trail, and Tonsina Point. I love them for different reasons. Carter Lake is perfect if you're looking to hike up into a mountain getaway and be surprised by a beautiful mountain lake. If you're lucky, you might also enjoy one of Alaska's rare thunderstorms while you're out there (At least, that was my experience last time)! On the other hand, Tonsina Point offers a gorgeous temperate rainforest hike followed up by a beach camping experience, and with a little luck and good timing, delicious fresh caught salmon.

Fantasy and Sci-Fi Literature

While I enjoy reading poetry or literary classics every now and then, what really captures my attention are titles like Neil Gaiman's American Gods or Tolkien's The Hobbit. My top 10 favorite novels are:

  1. Ready Player One - Ernest Cline
  2. The Name of the Wind - Patrick Rothfuss
  3. Reamde - Neal Stephenson
  4. The Hobbit - J.R.R. Tolkien
  5. American Gods - Neil Gaiman
  6. NEXT - Michael Crichton
  7. Old Man's War - John Scalzi
  8. Ender's Game - Orson Scott Card
  9. Neverwhere - Neil Gaiman
  10. Good Omens - Neil Gaiman & Terry Pratchet

I know that some people argue Ready Player One was too crammed full of pop-culture references, but looking past that, it's a really great story with a touching spin about what life might be like in the not too distant future. Here's hoping Steven Spielberg does a good job with it in the upcoming movie! Top title aside, out of this list my most recent read was American Gods. I honestly wasn't sure I was going to care for the novel at first, but it's hard hitting punches and amazing finish blew me away! If you're getting through the book right now and finding it a bit slow, just wait until you get to the last part of the book, it's fantastic.
